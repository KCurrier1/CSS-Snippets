Where I came from...

I was born and raised in the small farm town of Blythe, California. There was very little opportunity to do much of anything there so I worked hard to get a good GPA in highschool where I could further my education at Grand Canyon University. I was apart of numerous clubs as well as captaining our Marching Band's small Colorguard team. My mother is the Chief Nursing Officer of our local hospital, and my dad has bounced through many maintenance jobs and has ultimately retired due to his health. We have not always been this well off, and my mom has worked hard to be where she is. We don't have the best relationship but I do look up to her as someone who knows how to put in effort to get what they want. I aspire to do the same, despite not exactly following in her footsteps.

How I describe myself...
As a Woman in Tech...

I was a Computer Programming Major for two years before switching to the new Bachelors of Science in Software Development degree. It is quite apparent that this is not a very "feminine" topic to study. This was very intimidating in the beginning but is also a big source of motivation for me. I aim to persevere and become a mentor for girls who want to pursue a career in this field. Although, since I have had time to develop a lot of my skills so far, I have taken a liking to more of the design aspect of website building. However, I don't want to be categorized as simply a designer, because it seems that is where many women are funneled into and get stuck. I want to prove that I can program from frontend to backend, without taking shortcuts on my database layers or turning in ugly looking website designs. I believe with a bit of determination and hard work, I can do it all well.
